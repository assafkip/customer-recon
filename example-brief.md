# Example: filled-in brief

This is what the system produces. The market and company are invented. The shape is real.

Company: a tool that automates bookkeeping for freelance designers. Buyer: solo creative freelancers who hate admin.

---

## Watering holes (Prompt 1 output)

On-target: r/freelance, r/graphic_design (the business threads), r/Entrepreneur (only the "I hate invoicing" posts), and two Discord communities for design freelancers.

Surprise: r/ADHD. A lot of freelance designers there talk about bookkeeping as an executive-function wall, not a tooling problem. Different angle, same buyer.

Skip: r/smallbusiness. Too broad, mostly not creatives.

## Voice of customer (Prompt 2 output, sample)

- "I will literally take a lower-paying client over doing my own books for a week." (r/freelance, resigned)
- "QuickBooks is built for businesses with an accountant. I am one guy with a Wacom." (r/graphic_design, annoyed)
- "I have three years of receipts in a shoebox and tax season gives me panic attacks." (r/ADHD, desperate)

## Pain map (Prompt 3 output)

1. Tax-season dread (frequent, dealbreaker). "Panic attacks." Top of the list.
2. Tools built for accountants, not solo creatives (frequent, real cost).
3. Chasing late invoices (occasional, real cost).
4. Categorizing expenses (frequent, mild annoyance).

Lead with tax-season dread. Ignore expense categorizing as a headline.

## Competitor read (Prompt 4 output)

QuickBooks: praised for being complete, hated for being overkill and confusing for one-person shops. FreshBooks: liked for invoicing, "still feels like homework." Spreadsheets: the real incumbent, free, and everyone admits it falls apart at tax time.

The gap across all of them: none of them remove the dread. They give you a better place to do the work you are avoiding. Nobody does the work for you.

## The objection (Prompt 5 output)

Buyer to a colleague: "Another subscription to forget about. I already pay for FreshBooks and barely open it."

The status quo wins because new tools become one more thing to manage.

## The wedge (Prompt 6 output)

**Lead with:** tax-season dread. Quote: "tax season gives me panic attacks."

**Wedge:** every option gives you a better tool to do the avoidance work. None remove it. Position as "it does the books, you do the design," not "better bookkeeping software."

**Their words:** "shoebox of receipts," "one guy with a Wacom," "feels like homework." Use these. Not "streamline your finances."

**Objection + counter:** "one more subscription I'll forget." Counter: it runs without you opening it. The proof has to be that they can ignore it and it still works.

**Engage honestly in:** the r/ADHD executive-function thread, the r/freelance "I hate invoicing" post, the design Discord admin channel. Be useful. Do not pitch.
